Abstract

Melanoma incidence has increased by 46.6% over the past decade, yet early detection remains limited by dermatologist shortages and the visual subtlety of early-stage lesions. We systematically evaluate five architectures spanning custom CNNs, pretrained CNNs, Transformers, and vision-language models on the HAM10000 dermoscopy dataset for 7-class skin disease classification and weakly supervised lesion localization.

Hyperparameters for supervised models are selected via sequential ablation rather than exhaustive grid search. Zero-shot CLIP and DermLIP are evaluated across seven prompt templates. Grad-CAM heatmaps and self-attention maps are compared against ground truth segmentation masks using Intersection over Union (IoU) and Dice score to quantify spatial localization quality.

Fine-tuned ResNet-50 achieves the strongest classification and localization performance, while zero-shot VLMs demonstrate competitive accuracy on majority classes but fail to spatially ground lesion regions — revealing that classification accuracy and spatial grounding are dissociable.

↯
Central Finding Zero-shot VLMs can achieve competitive global classification accuracy while producing near-zero IoU against lesion masks — demonstrating that correct predictions do not imply spatial understanding.
Methods

Five Models. Three Paradigms.

From Scratch
Custom CNN
4-block conv architecture with BatchNorm, GAP, and Dropout. Baseline measuring the cost of pretraining.
Grad-CAM: block4[0]
Fine-tuned
ResNet-50
ImageNet pretrained. FC replaced with Dropout(0.4) + Linear(2048→7). Full network fine-tuned end-to-end.
Grad-CAM: layer4[-1]
Fine-tuned
Swin-T
Swin Transformer Tiny via timm. Window-based self-attention. Dropout injected at head.drop.
Grad-CAM: layers[-1].blocks[-1].attn
Zero-Shot
CLIP ViT-B/16
OpenAI CLIP pretrained on 400M internet image-text pairs. Zero-shot via cosine similarity with class prompts.
Attn: resblocks[-1] CLS→patch
Zero-Shot
DermLIP ViT-B/16
CLIP fine-tuned on Derm1M — 1M dermatology image-text pairs across 390 skin conditions.
Attn: resblocks[-1] CLS→patch

Grounding Evaluation

All heatmaps binarized at τ = 0.5 and compared against ground truth segmentation masks from HAM10000 using IoU and Dice score.

IoU = |H ∩ M| / |H ∪ M|
Dice = 2|H ∩ M| / (|H| + |M|)
VLM Attention: Standard Grad-CAM fails for CLIP/DermLIP because encode_image pools through the CLS token, leaving patch gradients zero. Instead, CLS-to-patch self-attention weights are extracted from the final resblock, averaged over heads, and reshaped to a 14×14 spatial grid.
